Theory

A self-fulfilling prophecy occurs when the expectations that appearance creates are realized. Chubby cheeks, a big forehead, or a soft jawline may lead to social interactions that elicit “babyish” or playful behavior. The behavior can then have an influence on the individual, creating a self-fulfilling prophecy. People may acquire the psychological traits that their facial appearance led others to expect.

A self-defeating prophecy effect occurs when the expectations that appearance creates produce effects that elicit behavior just the opposite of what is expected. Often, the target of the personality expectancies defeats them because the social interactions produced by the expectancies are negative. For example, the chubby-cheeked boy who is expected to be innocent may reject this childlike image and compensate by rebelling.
